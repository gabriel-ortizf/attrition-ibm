## Overview

The organization is losing **16.1% of its workforce annually**. At an industry-standard replacement cost of 1.5× annual salary, this translates to an estimated **$27.7M in avoidable costs** — with 226 current employees already in the high-risk zone.

This project goes beyond describing attrition. It delivers a validated risk score that allows HR to identify at-risk employees *before* they resign, and three prioritized recommendations with quantified business impact.

**Three questions this analysis answers:**
1. Who is leaving? (demographic and role profile)
2. Why are they leaving? (validated driver analysis via logistic regression)
3. Who is next? (Friction Index risk scoring)

## Methods

### 1. Descriptive Analysis
Attrition rates by job role, department, and marital status. Numeric profile comparison (age, income, tenure, distance) between employees who stayed vs. those who left.

### 2. Driver Analysis — Logistic Regression
Logistic regression with standardized coefficients using `statsmodels`. Standardization allows direct comparison of effect sizes across variables on different scales. Results reported as standardized coefficients and Odds Ratios (OR) with 95% confidence intervals and p-values.

**Model fit:** Pseudo R² (McFadden) = 0.225 · AIC = 884.1 · All predictors p < .05

### 3. Compounding Effects
Cross-tabulation of the three highest-impact factor combinations:
- Overtime × Marital Status
- Age Group × Income Level
- Overtime × Income Level

Combinations selected based on the top predictors identified by the regression model.

### 4. Friction Index
A composite risk score built from the standardized regression coefficients:

```
Friction Index = 0.74×Overtime + 0.45×Single + 0.32×Companies + 0.27×Distance
               − 0.48×Income − 0.48×YearsInRole − 0.39×Age
```

Variables normalized before combining. Weights are the regression coefficients — not arbitrary — so each factor's contribution is proportional to its validated predictive power.

**Validation:** Red zone (top 25%) attrition rate = 38.6% vs. 8.6% outside. AUC = 0.767.

## Key Findings

| Finding | Result |
|---|---|
| Overall attrition rate | 16.1% |
| Strongest risk factor | Overtime (OR = 2.11, p < .001) |
| Single + Overtime attrition | 49.6% |
| Under-30 + Low income attrition | 35.1% |
| Low income + Overtime attrition | 53.3% |
| Friction Index red zone rate | 38.6% vs 8.6% (4.5× multiplier) |
| Employees currently in red zone | 226 |
| Estimated cost if red zone leaves | $26.5M |

## Three Prioritized Recommendations

**Priority 1 — Staffing audit in Sales**
Sales Representatives (39.8% attrition) are the highest-risk role. Structural overtime in this role is the primary driver. A headcount analysis to reduce chronic overwork yields the highest ROI.

**Priority 2 — Pay review for junior roles**
Employees under 30 with low income leave at 35.1%. A targeted pay-parity review for entry-to-mid-level positions directly addresses the income component of the Friction Index.

**Priority 3 — Stay interviews for red-zone employees**
226 current employees score above the risk threshold, with Research Scientists showing the highest concentration (29.4%). Proactive retention conversations before the resignation decision are the most cost-effective intervention.
