02 / Why are they leaving?

Rates tell us where.
Regression tells us why.

Descriptive rates show correlation, not causation. To understand independent drivers, controlling for everything else simultaneously, we use logistic regression with standardized coefficients.

The Odds Ratio (OR) tells us how much each factor multiplies the probability of leaving. OR = 2.1 means the employee is 2.1× more likely to leave.

Individual predictors are informative. But attrition rarely has a single cause. Some employees carry several risk factors simultaneously. When they stack, the numbers become alarming.

04 / The Friction Index

Can we score every employee's
attrition risk right now?

Individual predictors are useful but fragmented. A composite score, built from the regression coefficients, enables HR to rank current employees by risk and intervene before they resign.

Friction Index = 0.74×Overtime + 0.45×Single + 0.32×Companies + 0.27×Distance − 0.48×Income − 0.48×YearsInRole − 0.39×Age
These weights come directly from the standardized regression coefficients in Section 02 — the same model that showed overtime has a coefficient of 0.74, Single of 0.45, and so on. Each factor's contribution to the index is proportional to its validated predictive power. This is not a scoring formula built by intuition — it is the regression model repackaged as a practical tool.

06 / Strategic recommendations

Three actions.
One clear priority order.

The business case is clear. The question is where to act first for the highest return on retention investment.

Priority 1
Staffing audit
in Sales
Sales Representatives have a 39.8% attrition rate driven by structural overtime. A headcount analysis to eliminate chronic overwork in this role has the highest impact per dollar invested. This is a staffing problem, not a compensation problem.
Priority 2
Pay review for
junior roles
Employees under 30 with low income leave at 35.1%. A targeted pay-parity review for entry-to-mid-level positions, not a broad raise, directly addresses the income component of the Friction Index where it matters most.
Priority 3
Stay interviews
for red zone
226 current employees score above the risk threshold. HR should prioritize stay interviews and career development conversations with this group, especially Research Scientists (29.4% in red zone), before they reach the resignation decision.
